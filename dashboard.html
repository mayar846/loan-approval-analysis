Exhibit A

The Credit Cliff

CIBIL score does not correlate gently with approval — it acts as a near hard cutoff at 650. Below it, approval is rare. At or above it, rejection is rare.

Below 650 650 and above 10.6% ≈99%

Reading: the histogram above shows applicant counts by CIBIL score, split by outcome. Below score 650, rejections (rust) dominate almost completely. From 650 upward, approvals (green) take over just as completely — there is almost no transition zone.

Factors That Don't Move the Needle

Three commonly assumed factors in lending — education, self-employment, and number of dependents — show almost no variation in approval rate.

Exhibit C

Loan Term Effect

Shorter loan terms show a modest edge in approval rate, though the effect is far smaller than CIBIL score's and noisier across the range.

Exhibit D

Predictive Model Results

A Random Forest classifier trained on all applicant features reaches 97.9% accuracy on held-out data — and confirms, independently of the manual analysis above, that CIBIL score is doing almost all of the work.
